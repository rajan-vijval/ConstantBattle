STORIES

"I got kicked out of the homeless shelter a few days ago. I don't really like it over there though; they're all so violent and it's crowded. Sometimes I stay over here, where I can find some shade. I sleep in alleys or pretty much anywhere away from people. But they're violent over here too.

I'm 58 years old and former military police. After I got out I got a job as a housekeeper but I had to leave. I beat up a guy 'cause I didn't like the way he was talking to me. Around then I found out my father was doing drugs and drinking alcohol. That would've made a lot of people mad but that made me real mad 'cause I'm a preacher's son. So I started drinking and drugging too.

I don't know what to do now. No one will give me a job 'cause of my addiction. I don't have no family left. My wife and I divorced a long time ago and my mother's dead too. I've been to drug and alcohol programs from the VA but it hasn't worked out. They want you to help yourself. The longest I stayed clean was 2 years but I keep coming back. Out here on the streets I do it all the time.

Right now I'm just living day to day. I can't afford to think too far ahead. I don't know about the winter. I'll drive myself crazy. So now I'll probably just stay here until I can figure out what to do with the rest of my day. Maybe wait for the sun to go down so I can find somewhere to sleep."
